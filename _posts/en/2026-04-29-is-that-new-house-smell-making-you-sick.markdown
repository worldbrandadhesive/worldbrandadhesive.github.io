## What the Research Shows

Formaldehyde is colourless, fast-evaporating, and present in concentrations that build up quickly in enclosed spaces. Symptoms of exposure include:

- Burning eyes, nose, and throat
- Persistent headaches and fatigue
- Respiratory irritation, especially in children and the elderly
- Long-term carcinogenic risk at sustained higher levels

New homes and freshly renovated spaces are particularly vulnerable because multiple formaldehyde-emitting materials (flooring adhesives, furniture glue, wall panels) are all releasing gases simultaneously.

## The Role of Adhesives

Adhesives are one of the primary sources of formaldehyde emissions in interior spaces. Traditional contact adhesives and woodworking glues rely on formaldehyde-based resins as binders and preservatives. Every bond line in your flooring, cabinetry, and furniture is a potential source.

The good news: the formaldehyde is in the chemistry, not the function. High-performance, formaldehyde-free alternatives exist and are proven to bond just as strongly.
